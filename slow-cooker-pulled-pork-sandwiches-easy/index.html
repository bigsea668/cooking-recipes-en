Choosing the Best Pork for Slow Cooker Pulled Pork

The foundation of any great pulled pork sandwich is, of course, the pork itself. But which cut is best for slow cooking? While you can use leaner cuts, you'll generally get better results with something that has more marbling, as it will stay moist during the long cooking process.

Pork Shoulder (Boston Butt)

This is the gold standard for pulled pork. Pork shoulder, often called Boston butt (though it doesn't actually come from the butt!), is a relatively inexpensive cut with plenty of fat and connective tissue. This breaks down beautifully during slow cooking, resulting in incredibly tender and flavorful pork. Expect a cook time of 8-10 hours on low, or 4-6 hours on high.

Pork Picnic Shoulder

Similar to the Boston butt, the picnic shoulder is another excellent choice. It's generally a bit cheaper, but it may have skin on it, which you'll need to remove before cooking. It also benefits from a longer cooking time, around 8-12 hours on low.

Avoiding Lean Cuts

While you can technically use pork loin, it's not recommended. Lean cuts tend to dry out in the slow cooker, even with added liquid. If you do use pork loin, be sure to baste it frequently with the cooking liquid.

Easy Slow Cooker Pulled Pork Recipes

Now, let's get to the good stuff – the recipes! These slow cooker pulled pork recipes are designed to be simple, flavorful, and require minimal hands-on time. We'll start with a classic recipe and then explore some variations.

Classic Slow Cooker Pulled Pork

Ingredients: * 3-4 lb pork shoulder (Boston butt) * 2 tbsp brown sugar * 1 tbsp paprika * 1 tbsp garlic powder * 1 tbsp onion powder * 1 tsp cumin * 1 tsp chili powder * 1 tsp salt * 1/2 tsp black pepper * 1 cup chicken broth * 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar * 1/4 cup Worcestershire sauce

Instructions:

  • Combine brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Rub the mixture all over the pork shoulder.
  • Place the pork shoulder in the slow cooker.
  • Pour in the chicken broth, apple cider vinegar, and Worcestershire sauce.
  • Cook on low for 8-10 hours, or on high for 4-6 hours, until the pork is fork-tender.
  • Shred the pork with two forks.
  • Mix the shredded pork with the cooking liquid. Serve on buns with your favorite toppings.

    • Spicy Chipotle Slow Cooker Pulled Pork

    For a kick, try adding chipotle peppers in adobo sauce to the classic recipe. Add 1-2 chopped chipotle peppers and 1-2 tablespoons of adobo sauce to the cooking liquid. This adds a smoky heat that complements the pork beautifully.

    Carolina-Style Slow Cooker Pulled Pork

    Carolina-style pulled pork is known for its vinegar-based sauce. Omit the brown sugar and Worcestershire sauce from the classic recipe and increase the apple cider vinegar to 1 cup. Add 1/4 cup of red pepper flakes for a little heat. This results in a tangy, flavorful pulled pork that's perfect for a summer barbecue.

    Perfecting Your Pulled Pork Sandwich: Sauces & Sides

    Once you've mastered the art of slow cooking pulled pork, it's time to elevate your sandwich game. The right sauce and sides can take your meal from good to unforgettable.

    Choosing the Right BBQ Sauce

    While you can use store-bought BBQ sauce, making your own is surprisingly easy and allows you to customize the flavor to your liking. Consider a Kansas City-style sauce (sweet and smoky), a Texas-style sauce (spicy and savory), or a Carolina-style sauce (vinegar-based). Don't be afraid to experiment!     Delicious Side Dishes

    Pulled pork sandwiches pair well with a variety of side dishes. Classic choices include coleslaw, baked beans, potato salad, and mac and cheese. For a healthier option, try a side of grilled vegetables or a fresh salad. You can also explore more [easy dinner recipes](easy-dinner-recipes-busy-weeknights) for complementary sides.

    Bun Selection Matters

    Don't underestimate the importance of a good bun! A sturdy bun that can hold up to the juicy pork is essential. Brioche buns, potato rolls, or Kaiser rolls are all excellent choices. Toasting the buns lightly will help prevent them from getting soggy.

    Tips for the Best Slow Cooker Pulled Pork

    Here are a few extra tips to ensure your slow cooker meals for pulled pork sandwiches are a success:

    * Don't skip the rub: A good rub adds layers of flavor to the pork. Be generous with it! * Don't overcrowd the slow cooker: If your pork shoulder is too large, cut it in half to ensure even cooking. * Don't lift the lid unnecessarily: Each time you lift the lid, you release heat and moisture, which can prolong the cooking time. * Shred while warm: Pork is easier to shred when it's still warm. * Skim the fat: After cooking, skim off any excess fat from the cooking liquid before shredding the pork. This will result in a leaner, more flavorful pulled pork.

    ❓ FAQ

    How long does it take to cook pulled pork in a slow cooker?

    Typically, a pork shoulder takes 8-10 hours on low or 4-6 hours on high in a slow cooker. The exact time will depend on the size of the pork shoulder and your slow cooker.

    Can I use a different type of liquid besides chicken broth and apple cider vinegar?

    Absolutely! You can experiment with different liquids like beer, root beer, cola, or even orange juice. Each will impart a unique flavor to the pork.

    How do I store leftover pulled pork?

    Leftover pulled pork can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. You can also freeze it for up to 3 months. Reheat gently in the microwave or on the stovetop.
